Developing Biodegradable Solutions to Lobster Pots

Focusing on escape mechanisms for lobsters/fish to prevent ghost fishing and reduce micro-plastic pollution.

In this experiment, we are using PHA (polyhydroxyalkanoate), a fully biodegradable plastic that serves as an alternative to PLA. Despite its potential, PHAâ€™s applications in marine settings remain largely unexplored. We are developing a prototype for an escape mechanism that can be integrated with existing lobster pots with minimal modifications, reducing costs and simplifying the transition to sustainable fishing methods. Our approach involves 3D printing the model to offer an affordable production process, and we are currently assessing the strength and durability of the solution in real ocean conditions.
