Undergraduate (BSc Fisheries & Marine Sciences)

I earned my Bachelor’s (Honors) degree in Fisheries and Marine Sciences, specializing in Fisheries Science and graduating with First Class Honors (Danish scale GPA of 10.7). During my studies, I acquired comprehensive knowledge of fish biology—including physiology, histology, molecular genetics, and genetic studies—as well as introductory statistical modeling for fisheries and aquaculture, capture fisheries and fishing gear technology in the Indian Ocean region, and biological, chemical, and physical oceanography with a particular emphasis on fisheries oceanography. The curriculum also incorporated fundamental mathematics (calculus), enhancing my analytical skills. Throughout the program, I gained extensive hands-on experience in both laboratory and fieldwork contexts, particularly in histological and physiological experimentation. My Bachelor’s thesis focused on fisheries oceanography and involved mapping the fishing grounds of yellowfin tuna, bigeye tuna, and swordfish in relation to the spatial and temporal variability of the Indian Ocean. This research underscored my ability to integrate data analysis and environmental factors to tackle real-world challenges in fisheries management.

Assistant Lecturer (Oct 2021 - May 2023)

I joined the Department of Fisheries and Aquaculture as an Assistant Lecturer, engaging in teaching core modules on fisheries, aquaculture techniques, and marine conservation. I also supervised practical sessions and supported departmental research.

Master Student (Sep 2023 - Present)

Pursuing a Master's degree at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU Aqua), focusing on Fisheries, Biodiversity, and Resource Management. This is where I'm integrating Computer Vision and AI to address modern challenges in fisheries research and sustainability.

Student Assistant (Nov 2023 - Oct 2024)

Served as a student assistant in the Section of Fisheries Technology at DTU Aqua, assisting in data collection, lab experiments, and analyzing real-time data for ongoing fisheries research projects.

Academic Employee (Grade 2) from Oct 2024

After demonstrating a strong foundation and research aptitude, I was promoted to an Academic Employee (Grade 2) position at DTU Aqua, continuing to expand on research, international collaborations, and advanced studies in sustainable fisheries.
