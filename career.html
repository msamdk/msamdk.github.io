BSc Fisheries & Marine Sciences

I earned my Bachelor’s (Honors) degree in Fisheries and Marine Sciences, specializing in Fisheries Science, graduating with First Class Honors. During my studies, I acquired a comprehensive knowledge of fish biology including physiology, histology, molecular genetics, and genetic studies along with introductory statistical modeling for fisheries and aquaculture, capture fisheries and fishing gear technology in the Indian Ocean region, as well as biological, chemical, and physical oceanography. The curriculum also included fundamental mathematics (calculus), strengthening my analytical skills.

Throughout the program, I gained hands-on experience in laboratory and fieldwork contexts—particularly in histological and physiological experimentation. My Bachelor’s thesis focused on fisheries oceanography, where I mapped the fishing grounds of yellowfin tuna, bigeye tuna, and swordfish in relation to spatial and temporal variability in the Indian Ocean.

Assistant Lecturer (Oct 2021 - May 2023)

I served as an Assistant Lecturer in the Department of Fisheries and Aquaculture, Faculty of Fisheries and Marine Sciences & Technology, University of Ruhuna, Sri Lanka. There i had the opportunity to teach undergraduate students of Level 2 and level 3 on Statistics, capture fisheries and fisheries oceanography. I assistated in organizing and designing practical sessions for the undergraduate students.

Master Student (Sep 2023 - July 2025)

Finished my Masters in Engineering from Technical University of Denmark. My Thesis was about Re-Identification of fish using computervision and deep learning techniques for the goal of smart fisheries in the future. In here i experimented Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Vision Transformers (ViT) for the Re-ID task. This was a wonderful research experience and a great 2 year period of my life.

Student Assistant (Nov 2023 - July 2025)

I worked as a student assistant for the section of Fisheries Technology, DTU Aqua from November 2023 to October 2024 and then upgraded as an Academic Employee (AC-TAP) grade 2. During this period of almost 2 years, I have participated in data collections, data annotations for computer vision applications in fisheries, especially with the PhD students of the section. I have had the opportunity to work closely with senior researchers on their projects as well. For example, the biodegradable material for fishing nets testing, designing and building structures for teaching purposes, and also some video animation production for the offshore wind farms (Look in the personal projects).
